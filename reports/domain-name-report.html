Domain Name Research

Research Questions

What is an internet domain name?
Its the address used to find a website, like google.com. Its a stand in for the numerical IP address that computers actually use.

Why are domain names so important on the web?
They make websites easy to remember and to go to, they also help make a brand or identity online. Without them, people would have to memorize long strings of numbers.

Why do domain names have to be unique?
Every domain goes to a specific website, so having many of the same one wouldn't work and would break the internet's ability to guide users to the right place.

What does it mean to "register" a domain name?
It means paying a registrar to reserve the rights to use that domain for a set period (usually 1+ years). You don't own it forever — you renew it to keep it.

If you wanted a domain name that is already taken, how could you get it?
You can contact the current owner and ask to buy it, or you could use a broker or marketplace, or wait for it to expire and get it then.
