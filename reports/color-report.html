Report 01 · Color & Mood

Color & Mood for Under the Sky

Research

Warm vs. cool colors

Warm colors (reds, oranges, yellows) feel energetic and inviting and visually advance toward the viewer. Cool colors (blues, greens, purples) feel calm and distant and recede. For a quiet photo gallery like Under the Sky, cool tones fit best, with small warm accents so the site doesn't feel cold.

Warm — energy, closeness
Cool — stillness, distance

Simultaneous contrast

The same color looks different depending on what surrounds it. Both gray squares below are identical — only the backgrounds change. Colors must always be tested in context, not picked in isolation.

Color and culture

Color meaning is not universal. White signals purity at Western weddings but mourning across much of East Asia. Red is danger in the West but luck and celebration in China. Purple reads as royalty in the West but mourning in Thailand. A small personal site doesn't need to solve this, but the lesson is to not assume a color "obviously" means what it means to you.
